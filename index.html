§ 02 / The Problem Where Margin Goes to Die

The most expensive problem you're not solving.

Your ops manager reviews T&M contracts between site visits. Your PM runs RFQs off the side of their desk. If you're the President, CEO, CFO, VP, or Director — this is hitting your bottom line whether you see it or not.

01

Cost leakage is invisible

Rate escalations buried in contract amendments. Duplicate vendor agreements. Blanket POs that haven't been reconciled in eighteen months. Industry benchmarks show 3–5% of construction spend is lost to invoice fraud, duplicate payments, and unauthorized purchasing alone. On a $50M project, that's $1.5M–$2.5M walking out the door.

02

Compliance is a full-time job

COR, ISN, WCB, Avetta, ComplyWorks, ContractorCheck — contractor compliance isn't optional, and it isn't simple. Add insurance and bonding requirements, Indigenous content commitments under IBAs, and AER regulations — and you've got a full-time job nobody's doing full-time.

03

Your people are stretched too thin

Once you cross $20M–$100M in revenue, the owner or project manager can't handle purchasing on the side anymore. Procurement is its own discipline — with its own skill set, tools, and pitfalls. Asking your team to do it is like asking your PM to run your books. They can try. You won't like the results.

04

A full-time hire doesn't pencil

A procurement specialist in Alberta runs $90–120K base and $150–230K fully loaded. For most companies, that's hard to justify — especially when project loads fluctuate. You need the expertise without the overhead.

05

Supply chain risk is unmanaged

Post-COVID exposed companies running on single-source vendors for pipe, valves, and specialty trades. Some saw 6–18 month project delays in the oilsands. Without someone tracking your supply chain risk, you won't see the problem until it's already on your critical path.

06

You're leaving contracts on the table

Alberta Transportation, Alberta Infrastructure, and municipal RFPs score your procurement methodology. No documented SCM approach means you're losing points before your technical team even gets evaluated. Companies with formal procurement processes win more public work — period.

Your ops team vs. a dedicated procurement function
