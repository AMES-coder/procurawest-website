2. Value Proposition Canvas

CEO
Their Job Grow the company, manage risk, keep overhead lean, make sure projects deliver on budget.
Their Pain Procurement is buried under operations. Nobody owns it. Contracts get signed without proper review. Costs creep up and nobody catches it until it's too late. Hiring a full-time procurement manager is $155K+ in Alberta — hard to justify for a 200-person company.
Our Gain "You're running a business, not a procurement department." We give you a fully functioning procurement operation at a fraction of the cost of a hire. You get monthly reporting, spend visibility, and contract control — without adding headcount.

Ops Manager
Their Job Keep projects running, manage crews and contractors, hit deadlines. Procurement is one of 15 things on their plate — and it's the one they're least equipped for.
Their Pain They're comparing 3-4 supplier quotes because there's no time for more. They're signing T&M contracts without proper rate benchmarking. Vendor issues eat their time. They know procurement needs attention but they can't do their real job AND run a proper sourcing process.
Our Gain "We're not replacing you — we're your specialist teammate." We take procurement off their desk so they can run operations. We handle the RFQs, the vendor evaluations, the contract admin. They stay in the loop without doing the legwork.

Project Manager
Their Job Deliver the project on time, on budget. Coordinate subcontractors, manage schedules, keep the client happy.
Their Pain Material delays blow up timelines. Subcontractor performance issues derail milestones. Change orders stack up because contracts weren't tight enough. They're losing 2.5-5% of total project value because nobody has time to run a proper sourcing process.
Our Gain "We keep the supply chain tight so your project stays on track." We manage vendor performance, track deliverables, and make sure contracts are written to protect you — not just close the deal.

CFO
Their Job Control costs, forecast accurately, reduce financial risk, report to the board.
Their Pain They can see costs are higher than they should be, but they can't pinpoint where the leakage is. Maverick spending runs 25-80% of total spend in companies without procurement oversight. Off-contract purchases cost 10-20% more than negotiated rates. They're flying blind on vendor spend.
Our Gain "We'll show you exactly where the money's going." Monthly spend analysis, contract compliance tracking, and vendor consolidation. We turn unmanaged spend into managed spend — and every new dollar under management saves 6-12%.

4. Objection Handling

"Our ops team handles procurement fine."

Response: Your ops team is great at operations — that's their job. But procurement is a different discipline. When your ops manager is comparing 3-4 quotes because they don't have time for more, you're leaving 2.5-5% of project value on the table. That's not a knock on them — it's a math problem. You wouldn't ask your project manager to do your accounting. Same principle.

Proof point: Companies with professional procurement manage 91.5% of spend under contract vs. 57.1% for those without. That gap is where the money leaks out.

"We're too small to need a procurement consultant."

Response: Actually, smaller companies benefit most. A 200-person contractor spending $5M/year on subcontractors and materials? Even a 6% improvement from proper sourcing is $300K back in your pocket. That's real money — and it's exactly why we built our Foundation tier. You don't need a full procurement department. You need someone who knows what they're doing checking your contracts and running your RFQs properly.

Proof point: Every dollar placed under professional procurement control yields 6-12% savings (KPMG). On $5M in spend, that's $300K-$600K.

"We can't afford it."

Response: You can't afford not to have it. Here's the real cost comparison: a procurement manager in Calgary runs $150K-$230K/year fully loaded. Our monthly packages cost a fraction of that. And we typically find enough savings in the first 90 days to cover our fees several times over. The question isn't whether you can afford procurement expertise — it's how much you're losing without it. The industry average for contract leakage in unmanaged spend is 10-20%.

Proof point: If you're spending $3M/year on contractors and materials with no procurement oversight, you're likely losing $300K-$600K to maverick spending, rate creep, and missed negotiation opportunities.

"We've never outsourced procurement before."

Response: That's actually the norm for companies your size. You've never had a procurement department at all — your ops team just handles it on top of everything else. We're not asking you to outsource something that's working. We're offering to build something you've never had. Think of it like when you first hired a bookkeeper instead of doing your own books. You didn't outsource bookkeeping — you finally got a professional to do it right.

Proof point: We start with a procurement assessment — no commitment. We'll show you where your spend is going, where the gaps are, and what it's costing you. Then you decide.

"How do we know you understand our industry?"

Response: We've worked in Alberta oil & gas and construction our entire career. We know what a SAGD operator's procurement looks like. We know the difference between a well-managed T&M contract and one that's bleeding money. We know COR, ISN, WCB, ComplyWorks, Avetta, ISNetworld — not because we read about them, but because we've managed them. This isn't a generalist consultancy parachuting in from Toronto. We're Calgary-based, we know the players, and we know the work.

Proof point: Our client list is Alberta operators and contractors. We speak your language — POs, T&M rates, RFQs, subcontractor prequalification. Ask us a specific question about your procurement setup and you'll know in 30 seconds whether we get it.
